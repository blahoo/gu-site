# Building an Electric Vehicle from Scratch

The Warrig x1 started as a crazy idea during a late-night engineering session. What if we could build a small, efficient electric vehicle that could navigate urban environments with ease?

## The Challenge

Building an electric vehicle isn't just about putting a motor on wheels. It involves:

- Power management systems
- Lightweight frame construction  
- Battery optimization
- Control systems integration

![Engineering Components](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1581091226825-a6a2a5aee158?w=600&h=400&fit=crop)

## What We Learned

The biggest challenge wasn't the technical aspects - it was understanding that the greatest skill is learning how to learn. Every component required diving deep into new domains, from electrical engineering to materials science.

## Results

The Warrig x1 successfully demonstrated that small-scale electric vehicles could be both practical and fun. While not ready for mass production, it served as an excellent proof of concept for future autonomous vehicle projects.

The project taught me that betting on the future sometimes means building it yourself, one component at a time.
