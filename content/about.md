# About Me

I'm a computer engineering student with a deep interest in the intersection of systems software and machine learning. I like understanding how things work at a low level — from memory allocators to GPU kernels.

## Background

I grew up in Vancouver, BC, and moved to Waterloo to study Computer Engineering. Outside of school, I spend most of my time working on side projects, reading papers, and contributing to open source.

## What I'm Interested In

**Systems Programming** — I find low-level systems work genuinely fascinating. There's something satisfying about writing code that's close to the metal, where you have to think carefully about memory, concurrency, and performance.

**Compilers & Programming Languages** — I've been slowly working through the dragon book and building toy compilers. The theory of type systems and program analysis is endlessly interesting to me.

**ML Infrastructure** — Not the modeling side, but the engineering side: distributed training, efficient inference, experiment tracking, and the tooling that makes research reproducible.
