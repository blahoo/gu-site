# The Apocalypse Hackathon Experience

![Hackathon Scene](/images/apocsign.png)

Apoc wasn't just another hackathon - it was a 48-hour marathon that pushed the boundaries of what we thought possible in rapid prototyping and collaborative engineering.

## The Setup

- 200+ participants
- 48 hours of non-stop building
- Challenges spanning from autonomous drones to digital security
- Sleep optional (and mostly avoided)

## Our Project

We tackled autonomous navigation systems for emergency response scenarios. The idea was to create drones that could navigate disaster zones and provide real-time mapping for rescue operations.

![Drone Technology](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1473968512647-3e447244af8f?w=600&h=400&fit=crop)

## The Grind

The most intense part wasn't the coding or the engineering - it was the constant decision-making under pressure. Every hour counted, and every choice could make or break the project.

## Lessons Learned

Hackathons are less about the final product and more about the process. You learn to:

- Prioritize ruthlessly
- Collaborate under pressure
- Iterate rapidly
- Fail fast and recover faster

The real victory wasn't winning (though we placed well) - it was proving that diverse skills and rapid learning could create something meaningful in an impossibly short timeframe.
