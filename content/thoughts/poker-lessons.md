# What Poker Taught Me About Engineering

![Poker Table](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1606092195730-5d7b9af1efc5?w=600&h=400&fit=crop)

Sitting at a poker table for hours teaches you things about decision-making, risk management, and human psychology that no engineering textbook ever could.

## Expected Value vs. Outcome

In poker, you can make the right decision and still lose. In engineering, you can build the perfect system and still have it fail in production.

Both teach you to focus on process over results.

## Information Management

Poker players work with incomplete information and have to make optimal decisions anyway. Sound familiar?

Every engineering project is the same - you never have all the data you want, but you still need to ship.

## Risk vs. Reward

The best poker players aren't the ones who avoid risk - they're the ones who take calculated risks when the odds are in their favor.

Same with building things. The biggest mistake isn't taking risks, it's not taking enough of the right ones.

## Reading the Room

Poker teaches you to read people, to understand their motivations and predict their actions.

In engineering, your "opponents" are physics, complexity, and time. But the principles are the same - pay attention, adapt your strategy, and always be thinking one step ahead.

## The Grind

Both poker and engineering reward consistency over brilliance. Show up every day, make good decisions, and trust the process.

The flashy moves get the attention, but it's the fundamentals that pay the bills.
